Common Conditions

Conditions We Cover

India bears a significant burden of both communicable and non-communicable diseases. Below are high-prevalence conditions with evidence-based guidance.

Hypertension

Affects ~30% of Indian adults. Normal BP is below 120/80 mmHg. Stage 1 begins at 130/80. Risk factors include high salt intake, obesity, stress, and family history.

Home management: Reduce sodium to <5g/day, walk 30 mins daily, limit alcohol, practice deep breathing or yoga (Pranayama).

When to see a doctor: BP consistently above 140/90, headaches, blurred vision, or chest discomfort.

Call 104 — Health Helpline →

Type 2 Diabetes

India has 101 million diabetics (IDF 2023). Fasting glucose >126 mg/dL or HbA1c >6.5% confirms diagnosis. Often linked to sedentary lifestyle and genetic predisposition.

Home management: Prefer whole grains (ragi, jowar, bajra) over refined flour. Include bitter gourd (karela), methi seeds, and cinnamon in diet. Walk 45 mins daily.

When to see a doctor: Excessive thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, wounds that won't heal.

Call 104 — Health Helpline →

Asthma

Affects ~34.3 million Indians. Triggered by dust, pollen, cold air, pollution (AQI >200), and respiratory infections. Peak season: Oct–Feb in North India.

Home management: Keep reliever inhaler (Salbutamol) accessible always. Use steam inhalation with eucalyptus oil. Avoid incense/dhoop during flare-ups. Use N95 mask on high AQI days.

When to see a doctor: Inhaler needed >2 times/week, nighttime symptoms, difficulty completing sentences.

Call 108 — Ambulance (severe attack) →

Anxiety Disorders

~45 million Indians affected (WHO). Includes Generalised Anxiety Disorder (GAD), panic attacks, and social anxiety. Stigma remains a major barrier to treatment.

Self-help: Box breathing (4-4-4-4 technique), progressive muscle relaxation, limit caffeine/tea to 2 cups/day. Apps: Wysa (Indian mental health app), Headspace.

When to seek help: Anxiety persists >2 weeks, interferes with work/relationships, physical symptoms (heart racing, trembling).

Call iCall — 9152987821 →

Lower Back Pain

Affects ~60% of Indians at some point. Major causes: prolonged sitting (IT sector), poor posture, lifting heavy loads (manual labour), vitamin D deficiency.

Home management: Avoid prolonged bed rest — stay active. Apply warm compress for 20 mins, 3x/day. Cat-cow yoga stretches. Ensure 15 mins sun exposure daily for Vitamin D.

Red flags — see doctor immediately: Leg numbness/weakness, loss of bladder control, pain after a fall/injury, fever with back pain.

Call 104 — Health Helpline →

Migraine

Prevalence ~14% in India. More common in women (3:1 ratio). Triggered by irregular meals, dehydration, screen time, strong perfumes, weather changes, and menstrual cycle.

Home management: Maintain a trigger diary. Stay hydrated (3L/day). Apply cold compress on forehead. Drink ginger tea at onset. Rest in a dark, quiet room. Magnesium-rich foods (almonds, spinach).

When to see a doctor: >4 attacks/month, aura symptoms (visual disturbances), pain not relieved by OTC painkillers within 2 hours.

Call 104 — Health Helpline →
Symptoms

Check Your Symptoms

Don't know your condition? Start with what you're feeling. If any symptom appears in the "Immediately" column, call 112 or 108 without delay.

Symptom Common Causes When to Seek Care
Chest pain Acidity (GERD), muscle strain, angina, heart attack (MI) Call 112 if crushing, radiating to left arm/jaw, or with sweating & breathlessness
Shortness of breath Asthma, anxiety, COPD, pneumonia, heart failure Call 108 if sudden onset at rest, blue lips, or unable to speak in full sentences
Persistent fatigue Anaemia (iron/B12), thyroid disorder, diabetes, depression, dengue recovery See doctor within 1 week if lasting >4 weeks without clear cause. Blood test: CBC, TSH, HbA1c
Severe headache Tension, migraine, hypertension, sinusitis, meningitis Call 112 if sudden "thunderclap" onset, stiff neck, fever, confusion, or worst headache of life
High fever (>103°F / 39.4°C) Dengue, malaria, typhoid, COVID-19, UTI, viral infection Call 108 if with rash, bleeding gums, severe body pain, or altered consciousness. Get dengue NS1 test if in monsoon season
Unexplained weight loss Diabetes, hyperthyroidism, TB, malignancy, depression See doctor within 2 weeks if >5% body weight lost in 6 months. TB screening if with chronic cough
Severe abdominal pain Appendicitis, kidney stones, gallstones, gastric ulcer, pancreatitis Call 108 if sudden sharp pain, rigid abdomen, bloody stool/vomit, or fainting

Treatments

Treatment Pathways

From evidence-based home remedies to clinical interventions — understand your options. Always verify with a qualified doctor before starting any treatment.

Medications

Common OTC medicines (India):

  • Paracetamol (Dolo/Crocin): Fever & mild pain — 500mg, max 4g/day
  • Cetirizine: Allergies, runny nose — 10mg once daily
  • ORS (Electral/ORS WHO): Dehydration from diarrhoea/vomiting — 1 sachet in 1L water
  • Antacid (Gelusil/Digene): Acidity & heartburn — after meals

Never self-prescribe antibiotics. Antibiotic resistance is a serious crisis in India.

Home Remedies (Evidence-Based)

Remedies supported by clinical evidence:

  • Turmeric milk (Haldi doodh): Anti-inflammatory — 1 tsp turmeric + warm milk for sore throat, joint pain
  • Ginger tea (Adrak chai): Nausea relief, cold & cough — boil 1 inch ginger in water, add honey
  • Tulsi (Holy Basil): Respiratory infections — 5-6 leaves in boiling water as decoction
  • Ajwain water: Bloating & indigestion — 1 tsp ajwain boiled in 1 glass water
  • Honey + warm water: Sore throat, mild cough — 1 tbsp honey (do NOT give to infants <1 year)
  • Jeera (Cumin) water: Acidity & metabolism — soak 1 tsp overnight, drink in morning

Lifestyle & Yoga

AYUSH Ministry recommended practices:

  • Pranayama (Anulom Vilom): Stress, anxiety, mild hypertension — 10 mins/day
  • Surya Namaskar: Overall fitness — 12 rounds daily for cardiovascular health
  • Walking: Diabetes management — 45 mins brisk walking, 5 days/week
  • Diet (Indian plate method): ½ plate vegetables, ¼ whole grains (roti/brown rice), ¼ dal/protein
  • Sleep hygiene: 7–8 hrs, no screens 1 hr before bed, consistent schedule

Government Health Schemes

Free/subsidised healthcare in India:

  • Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY): Free treatment up to ₹5 lakh/year for eligible families — call 14555
  • Jan Aushadhi Kendras: Generic medicines at 50–90% lower cost — 10,000+ stores across India
  • eSanjeevani: Free telemedicine — esanjeevani.mohfw.gov.in
  • PHC/CHC: Free primary care at your nearest Primary Health Centre
Mental Health

Mental Health Support

Mental health is health. 1 in 7 Indians is affected by mental health conditions (NIMHANS). Help is available — reaching out is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Depression

Persistent sadness, loss of interest, sleep changes, fatigue lasting >2 weeks. India has ~57 million people with depression (WHO). It is treatable.

Self-help: Maintain daily routine, physical activity 30 mins/day, talk to someone you trust, use Wysa or YourDOST apps.

Professional help: Visit your nearest government hospital psychiatry OPD (free). Private consultation: ~₹500–1500.

Vandrevala Foundation — 1860-2662-345 →

Anxiety

Excessive worry, restlessness, rapid heartbeat, difficulty concentrating. Often co-occurs with depression. Common in students and working professionals.

Self-help: Box breathing (inhale 4s → hold 4s → exhale 4s → hold 4s). Limit caffeine. 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique. Journal 10 mins/day.

Free counselling: iCall (TISS) — 9152987821 (Mon–Sat, 8am–10pm)

Call iCall — 9152987821 →

Grief & Loss

Grief is natural after loss. Complicated grief lasts >12 months and interferes with daily function. Cultural mourning practices can be healing — allow yourself the process.

Support: Talk to family/community elders. Join bereavement support groups. Seek professional help if you feel "stuck" in grief.

Helpline: AASRA — 9820466726 (24/7)

Crisis & Suicide Prevention

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger or having suicidal thoughts — you are not alone. Help is available right now.

What to do: Stay with the person. Remove access to harmful means. Call a helpline below. Do NOT leave them alone.

Emergency

Emergency & Urgent Care

Know when to act. Every government hospital in India is legally required to provide free emergency treatment for the first 24 hours regardless of payment ability (MTP Act / Supreme Court directive).

Call 112 — National Emergency

Call immediately for:

  • Crushing chest pain or signs of heart attack
  • Stroke symptoms — FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 112
  • Severe breathing difficulty or choking
  • Heavy uncontrolled bleeding
  • Loss of consciousness or seizures
  • Suspected poisoning or overdose
  • Severe burns or major trauma/accident
  • Snakebite (get to hospital within 1 hour — anti-venom is time-critical)
📞 Call 112 Now →

Call 108 — Ambulance

Free government ambulance service available in most Indian states. Also call for:

  • Pregnancy complications or emergency delivery
  • Road traffic accident — do NOT move the victim (spinal injury risk)
  • High fever (>104°F) with confusion or convulsions
  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) — difficulty breathing, swelling of face/throat
  • Suspected dengue with bleeding signs (monsoon season)
📞 Call 108 Now →

Poison Control

National Poison Information Centre (AIIMS Delhi):

  • Helpline: 011-26593677 or 011-26589391
  • Available 24/7 — operated by AIIMS New Delhi
  • Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by a doctor
  • Do NOT give milk or water to an unconscious person
  • Bring the poison container/label to the hospital
  • Common in India: pesticide ingestion, rat poison, kerosene, medication overdose

Not Sure? Here's How to Decide

Quick triage guide:

  • Call 112/108: Life-threatening, loss of consciousness, severe bleeding, chest pain, breathing difficulty, stroke signs
  • Go to ER (within 1 hour): Fractures, deep cuts, severe pain, high fever with rash, snakebite
  • See doctor today: Persistent vomiting, moderate fever >2 days, new shortness of breath, severe headache
  • Schedule appointment: Chronic symptoms, routine follow-up, mild ongoing issues
  • Call 104: Health advice, nearest hospital info, general medical queries
